Event 3

Summary Report: “FCAJ GameDay - Battle of the 8 Teams”

Event Objectives

  • Create an interactive learning activity that helps participants review their Cloud and AWS knowledge through a game-based format.
  • Encourage teamwork, coordination and mutual support among team members.
  • Develop quick thinking, question-analysis and decision-making skills under time pressure.
  • Create a healthy competitive environment among participants in the First Cloud AI Journey program.
  • Help participants reinforce previously learned knowledge in a more engaging and memorable way than traditional theoretical study.
  • Strengthen connections among participants and create a relaxing atmosphere after technical learning and practice sessions.

Participants

  • Students and interns participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program.
  • A total of eight teams competed by working together to select question sets and complete the challenges.
  • The organizing team was responsible for explaining the rules, managing the rounds and calculating the scores.
  • The two teams with the highest final scores were declared the winners.

Key Highlights

FCAJ GameDay Opening

  • The event was organized as a knowledge competition among eight participating teams.
  • Before the competition began, the organizers explained the rules, the question-set selection process, the scoring system and the time limit for each challenge.
  • Each team needed to assign responsibilities among its members for reading questions, discussing possible answers and submitting the final choice.
  • The event combined competition and entertainment, encouraging participants to become more active, focused and enthusiastic.

Selecting the Question Sets

  • Each team was allowed to select the question set or category that it wanted to complete.
  • Every question set included questions of different difficulty levels, requiring participants to apply their knowledge of Cloud computing and AWS.
  • Selecting a question set depended not only on chance but also on the team’s ability to evaluate the strengths and knowledge of its members.
  • A suitable selection could help a team maximize its score, while a difficult question set could create additional pressure related to time and problem-solving.
  • The selection process added a strategic element to the game because teams needed to balance difficulty, confidence and potential points.

Cloud and AWS Knowledge Challenges

  • The game focused on knowledge related to Cloud computing and the AWS service ecosystem.
  • Teams needed to apply their foundational knowledge, memory and analytical thinking to identify the correct answers.
  • Some questions required participants to distinguish between the functions of different AWS services or select the most appropriate service for a particular situation.
  • In addition to technical knowledge, response speed was an important factor that affected each team’s result.
  • Questions with similar or potentially confusing options required team members to discuss carefully before making their final decision.
  • The challenges helped participants recognize whether they truly understood each service or had only memorized its name.

Teamwork and Coordination

  • Each team member could have different strengths in areas such as compute, storage, databases, networking or security.
  • Teams that performed well generally assigned members to handle the topics they understood best and quickly combined their opinions.
  • For difficult questions, clear communication helped teams avoid spending too much time debating or repeatedly changing their answers.
  • The activity demonstrated that individual knowledge is important, but effective collaboration is necessary to maintain consistent performance throughout the competition.
  • Team members also practiced listening, respecting different opinions and reaching a shared decision within a limited amount of time.

Score Competition

  • Team scores were updated throughout the game, creating an exciting and competitive atmosphere.
  • The score difference between teams could change after each question set, making the final result difficult to predict until the last rounds.
  • Teams that answered accurately while maintaining a consistent response speed gained an important advantage on the leaderboard.
  • Monitoring the scores encouraged teams to remain focused and motivated them to improve their performance.
  • After all rounds were completed, the organizers calculated the final results and identified the highest-scoring teams.

Announcing the Winning Teams

  • The two teams with the highest scores after completing all challenges became the winners of FCAJ GameDay.
  • Their results reflected a combination of technical knowledge, response speed, question-set selection strategy and teamwork.
  • The announcement created an exciting conclusion to the event and recognized the effort of the participants throughout the competition.
  • However, the most important value of the activity was not limited to the final ranking, but also included the knowledge and experience that every team gained during the game.

Key Takeaways

  • Game-based learning makes knowledge more memorable: answering questions in a competitive environment creates motivation to review and helps participants identify gaps in their knowledge.
  • Foundational AWS knowledge is essential: to answer quickly, participants need to understand the functions and use cases of AWS services instead of only memorizing their names.
  • Teamwork directly affects performance: a team with knowledgeable members may still lose points if its members cannot communicate and agree on an answer effectively.
  • Assigning tasks based on individual strengths saves time: each member should focus on the areas they understand best while supporting the team in other topics when necessary.
  • Speed must be balanced with accuracy: answering quickly can provide an advantage, but making decisions too hastily can lead to incorrect answers and lost points.
  • Question-set selection requires strategy: teams need to evaluate difficulty, available time and their own knowledge before making a selection.
  • Healthy competition encourages development: the leaderboard motivated teams to perform better while maintaining a spirit of interaction and mutual support.
  • Mistakes reveal knowledge gaps: incorrect answers help participants determine which topics require further review and practice.
  • Communication is important in both games and professional work: a correct idea is only valuable when it is communicated clearly and understood by the team at the right time.

Applying to Work

  • Review the functions and use cases of AWS services that appeared in questions my team could not answer correctly.
  • Create a summary table that groups AWS services into categories such as compute, storage, databases, networking, security and monitoring.
  • Assign internship project tasks according to the strengths of each team member instead of having every member work on the same task.
  • Use short and evidence-based discussions when the team needs to make a technical decision.
  • Compare different AWS solutions based on requirements, cost, security and scalability before selecting a service.
  • Avoid choosing a service only because it is familiar and determine whether it is truly suitable for the problem.
  • Record uncertain questions or concepts and verify them later using official AWS documentation.
  • Organize short quizzes or knowledge-review activities within the team before the final project presentation.
  • Apply time-management skills when troubleshooting and avoid spending too much time on one issue while ignoring other important tasks.
  • Remain calm when facing an unfamiliar question or technical problem, then analyze the available information before choosing a solution.
  • Treat incorrect answers as a form of knowledge assessment and use them to create a more suitable learning plan.
  • Work with team members in a spirit of healthy competition while remaining focused on the overall quality of the project.

Event Experience

Participating in FCAJ GameDay on June 20, 2026, was an enjoyable learning experience that differed from regular technical sharing sessions.

  • Instead of only listening to presentations, I directly participated in selecting question sets, discussing answers with my teammates and making decisions within a limited time.
  • Having eight teams compete at the same time created an energetic atmosphere and encouraged every participant to remain focused.
  • I found that the game format made reviewing Cloud and AWS knowledge more enjoyable, accessible and less theoretical.
  • Some questions appeared simple at first but included very similar answer choices, requiring the team to understand the actual purpose of each AWS service.
  • Difficult questions helped me realize that I still confused the functions and use cases of several services.
  • During team discussions, I noticed that having many different opinions could sometimes consume additional time, but it also helped the team examine a problem from multiple perspectives.
  • The event helped me understand the importance of assigning responsibilities. When each member focused on the area they understood best, the team could answer more quickly and accurately.
  • Watching the scores change throughout the competition created excitement, especially when the difference between teams became smaller after each round.
  • The two highest-scoring teams demonstrated strong coordination, solid knowledge and an effective question-selection strategy.
  • Although not every team could win, all participants had an opportunity to assess their knowledge, learn from incorrect answers and improve their teamwork skills.
  • The event also allowed participants in the First Cloud AI Journey program to interact and understand one another better after studying and working on technical projects together.
  • It was an effective activity for balancing learning, competition and entertainment during the internship program.

Some Event Photos

Overall, FCAJ GameDay - Battle of the 8 Teams was a highly interactive and engaging learning activity. By selecting question sets, competing for points and coordinating with teammates, I had the opportunity not only to reinforce my Cloud and AWS knowledge but also to develop communication, quick-thinking, time-management and teamwork skills. The event demonstrated that technical learning can become more engaging and effective when it is combined with games and healthy competition.
